Who am I?

Hi, I'm Laure D'Este, 35, living in Liège and currently working for the Customs and Excise General Administration of the FPS Finance (yes already). But not for long as I'll be joining the brand new team of functional analysts coached by Switchfully.

I'm pround to say that I'm quite organized, even in uncertain environments, and that I'm always eager to learn and discover new skills.

I have a five year's experience in the Belgian Public Services. As part of my experience at the Public Service of Wallonia, I arrived at the very beginning of the Belgian Kilometer Charging System. From Molenbeek, I was led to become the link between my management, the toll operator who won the public market and a brand-new team of 15 agents. Once the Molenbeek team has become more autonomous I was called back near my management in Namur in order to assist then on the daily management of this new project and I also took charge of the administrative team for the walloon road tax for vehicles.

This experience was very rewarding and formative. However, my responsibilities were not correlated to my salary scale. I then migrated to the FPS Finance 2 years ago, finally valuing my bachelor's degree (in management assistant FYI).

For 6 months I worked at the Personnel and Organization Service before joining the Customs and Excise Administration where I could specialize in a more concrete business field and regain managing tasks.

A year and a half ago, I decided to attend evening classes to become a software developer to open up some new perspectives, still at the FPS Finance, in a constantly evolving field.

Today, the digitalization of the FPS is taking off more and more and I have the ambition to respond to it by making the most of my professional experience (#SoonToBeFunctionlaAlanyst) with my future diploma as an analyst programmer.
